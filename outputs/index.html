Stop Loss Framework

1. ATR Dynamic Stop

Adapts to market volatility. Wider in volatile markets, tighter in calm ones.

Stop = Entry − (ATR × Multiplier)

2. Fixed % Stop

Simple and predictable. Good for beginners.

Stop = Entry × (1 − %)

Regime Playbook

Strong Uptrend

NDX is well above its 250-day average. Bull market confirmed. The system runs maximum TQQQ exposure — up to 88%. Buy every dip to SMA(20). Bollinger Band width determines position size.

Watch for: Extension >8% above SMA(20) or RSI >75 signals a potential short-term top.

Uptrend

Above the 250-day average but not extended. Standard bull market behaviour. Mean reversion strategies (buying dips) work best here. Normal position sizes.

Watch for: NDX approaching SMA(250) from above — could be the start of a regime flip.

Downtrend

NDX has broken below its 250-day average. Bear market territory. All long strategies are turned OFF. Momentum short strategies activate — the system switches to SQQQ.

Critical rule: Do not buy dips below SMA(250). The system disables long strategies for this reason.

Strong Downtrend

Deep bear market. NDX is far below its long-term average. Fear is high. The system scales up SQQQ position based on depth. In 2022, the system limited losses to -10.9% while TQQQ fell -79%.

Watch for: RSI <25 is extremely oversold. Often marks bottoms.

What is Apex Trade Lab?

This is a paper trading simulation — a virtual portfolio that trades TQQQ and SQQQ based on 7 algorithmic strategies. No real money is involved. The system makes one decision each day, 10 minutes before market close, and executes at the next day's open.

What is paper trading?

Paper trading means simulated trading with virtual money. It lets you test a strategy's performance without risking real capital. Think of it as a flight simulator for trading.

How to read the dashboard

The Current Signal card at the top tells you what the system is doing right now and why. The equity curve shows how the portfolio has performed over time compared to simply buying QQQ. The regime indicator tells you whether we are in a bull or bear market.

Inside the System — 7 Strategies Explained

The system runs 7 non-correlated strategies simultaneously. Each has different entry conditions, goals, and risk profiles. They combine to create a smoother equity curve than any single strategy alone.

🟢 Momentum LongTQQQ
When: Uptrend (above SMA250)
Entry: NDX breaks above the upper Bollinger Band while above SMA(20).
Goal: Capture strong upward momentum. Ride the trend.
Risk: Stops out if NDX closes below SMA(20).
Example: In a strong bull market, NDX pushes above the upper BB. The system goes heavily long TQQQ to maximise gains during the momentum phase.
🟢 Mean Reversion Long 1TQQQ
When: Uptrend
Entry: NDX pulls back to within 1% of SMA(20) from above.
Goal: Buy the dip during uptrends. Pullbacks to SMA(20) are buying opportunities.
Risk: Exits if NDX extends more than 3% above SMA(20).
Example: After a 2-3% pullback in a bull market, the system buys the dip, expecting NDX to bounce back to its trend.
🟢 Mean Reversion Long 2TQQQ
When: Uptrend
Entry: NDX drops below the lower Bollinger Band while still above SMA(250).
Goal: Buy deep pullbacks aggressively. The deeper the dip, the larger the position.
Risk: Exits when NDX recovers above the middle BB.
Example: A sharp 5%+ pullback in an uptrend. The system sees this as a sale, buying more aggressively.
🟢 Mean Reversion Long 3TQQQ
When: Near SMA(250)
Entry: NDX is within 3% of SMA(250) and shows a bullish reversal candle.
Goal: Catch bounces near the critical SMA(250) support level.
Risk: Exits if NDX extends more than 5% above SMA(20).
Example: NDX tests its 250-day average—a key level. A bullish candle here triggers a speculative long.
🔴 Mean Reversion ShortSQQQ
When: Uptrend (overextended)
Entry: NDX is more than 4% above SMA(20) AND above the upper BB.
Goal: Short-term contrarian trade. Fade extreme overextension.
Risk: Exits when NDX reverts back to SMA(20).
Example: After a massive rally, NDX is stretched too far above its average. The system takes a small short position expecting a pullback.
🔴 Momentum Short 1SQQQ
When: Downtrend (below SMA250)
Entry: NDX closes below SMA(250) and below the lower BB.
Goal: Ride the downtrend. Position size scales with depth below SMA(250).
Risk: Exits when NDX reclaims SMA(250).
Example: NDX breaks below its 250-day average—a major bearish signal. The system switches to SQQQ.
🔴 Momentum Short 2SQQQ
When: Downtrend
Entry: NDX bounced to SMA(20) but failed and closed back below it.
Goal: Short failed bounces in a bear market. These are bull traps.
Risk: Exits if NDX stays above SMA(20) for 3 consecutive days.
Example: In a downtrend, a rally to SMA(20) that fails is a classic short entry. The system catches these bear market rallies.
